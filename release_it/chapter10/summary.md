## ✅ Key Takeaways (5 Bullets, Staff Framing)

1. **Autoscaling is reactive, not proactive** — Design for peak +20% capacity upfront; autoscaling cannot react to sudden spikes faster than 2-6 minutes.

2. **Load shedding protects core functionality** — Reject excess traffic early with 503 + Retry-After; better to serve 80% well than 100% poorly.

3. **Connection pool exhaustion is the silent killer** — Under load, connection pools, thread pools, and memory exhaust before CPU; monitor these metrics.

4. **Retry storms amplify failures** — Always use exponential backoff + jitter; immediate retries cause self-inflicted DDoS.

5. **Circuit breakers prevent cascades** — Isolate failures; let failing services recover; don't call them until they're healthy.
